# Forgotten Group Policy Paths: Integrating hidden OU & Site Attacks into BloodHound

**Speaker:** Quentin Roland

## Abstract

The present session describes the research process that led to the integration of hidden GPO-based attack paths into BloodHound. More specifically, the presentation stems from two contributions made by the author to the BloodHound project, adding new compromise edges targeting Organizational Units, as well as new object types & edges related to Active Directory Sites. In both cases, the newly highlighted attack paths could result in high-impact privilege escalation scenarios, or domain compromise.

The presentation will be structured around the approach adopted by the author for their BloodHound contribution. In that respect, it does not only describe the new exploitation vectors targeting OUs and Sites, but also gives pointers as to how to discover and integrate new identity-based attack paths into BloodHound. Said approach can be divided into three steps: discover new, unmapped attack paths; enumerate them by implementing them into BloodHound; provide actionable tooling to exploit them.
